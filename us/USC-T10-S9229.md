# § 9229. Command: commissioned officers in certain designated categories

##### § 9229. Command: commissioned officers in certain designated categories

An officer designated as a medical, dental, veterinary, medical service, or biomedical sciences officer or as a nurse is not entitled to exercise command because of rank, except within the categories prescribed in subsection (a), (b), (c), (d), (e), (f), or (i) of [section 9067 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s9067), or over persons placed under his charge.

*([Aug. 10, 1956, ch. 1041](/us/act/1956-08-10/ch1041), [70A Stat. 531](/us/stat/70A/531), § 8579; [Pub. L. 85–861, § 1(156)](/us/pl/85/861/s1/156), (185), Sept. 2, 1958, [72 Stat. 1513](/us/stat/72/1513), 1533; [Pub. L. 96–513, title II, § 212(b)](/us/pl/96/513/tII/s212/b), Dec. 12, 1980, [94 Stat. 2885](/us/stat/94/2885); renumbered § 9229 and amended [Pub. L. 115–232, div. A, title VIII](/us/pl/115/232/dA/tVIII), §§ 806(b)(7), 809(a), Aug. 13, 2018, [132 Stat. 1832](/us/stat/132/1832), 1840.)*

In subsection (a), the words “Except as provided in [section 94 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s94)”, not contained in section 1169 of the Revised Statutes, but contained in the United States Code, are omitted as surplusage, since 10:94 deals exclusively with assignments. The words “except within the categories prescribed in section 8067(a)–(d) of this title” are substituted for the words “in the line or in other staff corps”. Air Force nurses and women medical specialists are not covered by subsection (a), since their command authority is specifically stated in subsection (b).

In subsection (b), the words “may exercise command only” are substituted for the words “shall not be entitled * * * to command except”. The words “by virtue of their rank” and “by competent authority” are omitted as surplusage. 10:166(e) (last 22 words of last sentence) is omitted as superseded by [section 8012(e) of this title](/us/usc/t10/s8012/e).

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Amendments

2018—[Pub. L. 115–232](/us/pl/115/232) renumbered [section 8579 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s8579) as this section and substituted “section 9067” for “section 8067”.

1980—[Pub. L. 96–513](/us/pl/96/513) substituted provision prohibiting an officer designated as a medical, dental, veterinary, medical service, or biomedical sciences officer or as a nurse from exercising command because of rank, except within the categories prescribed in section 8067(a) to (f) or (i) of this title, or over persons placed under his charge for provision prohibiting an officer designated as a medical, dental, veterinary, or medical service officer from exercising command because of rank, except within categories prescribed in section 8067(a) to (d) of this title, and authorizing an Air Force nurse or medical specialist to exercise command only within his category, or over persons placed under his charge.

1958—Subsec. (b). [Pub. L. 85–861](/us/pl/85/861) struck out “woman” before “medical specialist”, and substituted “his” for “her” in two places.
