# § 1140k. Definition of student with a print disability

##### § 1140k. Definition of student with a print disability

In this subpart, the term “student with a print disability” means a student with a disability who experiences barriers to accessing instructional material in nonspecialized formats, including an individual described in [section 121(d)(2) of title 17](/us/usc/t17/s121/d/2).

*([Pub. L. 89–329, title VII, § 771](/us/pl/89/329/tVII/s771), as added [Pub. L. 110–315, title VII, § 709(2)](/us/pl/110/315/tVII/s709/2), Aug. 14, 2008, [122 Stat. 3367](/us/stat/122/3367).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### References in Text

[Section 121(d) of title 17](/us/usc/t17/s121/d), referred to in text, was amended by [Pub. L. 115–261, § 2(a)(1)(D)](/us/pl/115/261/s2/a/1/D), Oct. 9, 2018, [132 Stat. 3667](/us/stat/132/3667), and no longer defines blind or other persons with disabilities. However, [section 121(d) of title 17](/us/usc/t17/s121/d), as amended, does define “eligible person” to include blind persons, persons with visual impairment or perceptual or reading disability, and persons with other physical disabilities that inhibit reading skills.
