# § 6325. Absence resulting from hostile action abroad

##### § 6325. Absence resulting from hostile action abroad

(1) incurred while serving abroad and resulting from war, insurgency, mob violence, or similar hostile action; and

(2) not due to vicious habits, intemperance, or willful misconduct on the part of the employee.

*(Added [Pub. L. 90–221, § 3(a)](/us/pl/90/221/s3/a), Dec. 23, 1967, [81 Stat. 671](/us/stat/81/671); amended [Pub. L. 96–54, § 2(a)(41)](/us/pl/96/54/s2/a/41), Aug. 14, 1979, [93 Stat. 383](/us/stat/93/383); [Pub. L. 99–399, title VIII, § 804](/us/pl/99/399/tVIII/s804), Aug. 27, 1986, [100 Stat. 883](/us/stat/100/883).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### References in Text

Section 408 of the Foreign Service Act of 1980, referred to in text, is classified to [section 3968 of Title 22](/us/usc/t22/s3968), Foreign Relations and Intercourse.

#### Amendments

1986—[Pub. L. 99–399](/us/pl/99/399) inserted sentence at end relating to alien employees.

1979—[Pub. L. 96–54](/us/pl/96/54) substituted provisions relating to leave charged to an account of an employee for absence, for provisions relating to leave charged to an account of any officer or employee for absence, and designated qualifying provisions as cls. (1) and (2).
