# § 1730. Uniforms of carriers

##### § 1730. Uniforms of carriers

Whoever, not being connected with the letter-carrier branch of the Postal Service, wears the uniform or badge which may be prescribed by the Postal Service to be worn by letter carriers, shall be fined under this title or imprisoned not more than six months, or both.

The provisions of the preceding paragraph shall not apply to an actor or actress in a theatrical, television, or motion-picture production who wears the uniform or badge of the letter-carrier branch of the Postal Service while portraying a member of that service.

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Amendments

1994—[Pub. L. 103–322](/us/pl/103/322) substituted “fined under this title” for “fined not more than $100” in first par.

1990—[Pub. L. 101–647](/us/pl/101/647) struck out “, if the portrayal does not tend to discredit that service” before period at end of second par.

1970—[Pub. L. 91–375](/us/pl/91/375) substituted “Postal Service” for “Postmaster General” before “to be worn” in first par.

1968—[Pub. L. 90–413](/us/pl/90/413) inserted provision exempting an actor or actress in a theatrical, television, or motion-picture production who wears the uniform or badge of the letter-carrier branch of the Postal Service from the penalties imposed by this section.
