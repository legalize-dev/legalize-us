# § 347a. Congressional declaration of policy regarding oleomargarine sales

##### § 347a. Congressional declaration of policy regarding oleomargarine sales

The Congress hereby finds and declares that the sale, or the serving in public eating places, of colored oleomargarine or colored margarine without clear identification as such or which is otherwise adulterated or misbranded within the meaning of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act [[21 U.S.C. 301](/us/usc/t21/s301) et seq.] depresses the market in interstate commerce for butter and for oleomargarine or margarine clearly identified and neither adulterated nor misbranded, and constitutes a burden on interstate commerce in such articles. Such burden exists, irrespective of whether such oleomargarine or margarine originates from an interstate source or from the State in which it is sold.

*([Mar. 16, 1950, ch. 61, § 3(a)](/us/act/1950-03-16/ch61/s3/a), [64 Stat. 20](/us/stat/64/20).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### References in Text

The Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act, referred to in text, is [act June 25, 1938, ch. 675](/us/act/1938-06-25/ch675), [52 Stat. 1040](/us/stat/52/1040), which is classified generally to this chapter. For complete classification of this Act to the Code, see [section 301 of this title](/us/usc/t21/s301) and Tables.

#### Codification

Section was not enacted as part of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act which comprises this chapter.
