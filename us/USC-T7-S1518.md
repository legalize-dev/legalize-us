# § 1518. “Agricultural commodity” defined

##### § 1518. “Agricultural commodity” defined

“Agricultural commodity”, as used in this subchapter, means wheat, cotton, flax, corn, dry beans, oats, barley, rye, tobacco, rice, peanuts, soybeans, sugar beets, sugar cane, tomatoes, grain sorghum, sunflowers, raisins, oranges, sweet corn, dry peas, freezing and canning peas, forage, apples, grapes, potatoes, timber and forests, nursery crops, citrus, and other fruits and vegetables, nuts, tame hay, native grass, hemp, aquacultural species (including, but not limited to, any species of finfish, mollusk, crustacean, or other aquatic invertebrate, amphibian, reptile, or aquatic plant propagated or reared in a controlled or selected environment), or any other agricultural commodity, excluding stored grain, determined by the Board, or any one or more of such commodities, as the context may indicate.

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Codification

[Pub. L. 110–234](/us/pl/110/234) and [Pub. L. 110–246](/us/pl/110/246) made identical amendments to this section. The amendments by [Pub. L. 110–234](/us/pl/110/234) were repealed by [section 4(a) of Pub. L. 110–246](/us/pl/110/246/s4/a).

#### Prior Provisions

A former section 1518, [act Feb. 16, 1938, ch. 30, title V, § 518](/us/act/1938-02-16/ch30/tV/s518), [52 Stat. 77](/us/stat/52/77), was transferred to [section 1519 of this title](/us/usc/t7/s1519) at the time of the renumbering of such section 518 of act Feb. 16, 1938, as section 519 by [act June 21, 1941, ch. 214, § 9](/us/act/1941-06-21/ch214/s9), [55 Stat. 256](/us/stat/55/256).

#### Amendments

2018—[Pub. L. 115–334](/us/pl/115/334) inserted “hemp,” before “aquacultural species”.

2008—[Pub. L. 110–246, § 12033(c)(2)(B)](/us/pl/110/246/s12033/c/2/B), substituted “this subchapter” for “this chapter”.

2000—[Pub. L. 106–224](/us/pl/106/224) struck out “livestock and” before “stored grain” and “under subsection (a) or (m) of [section 1508 of this title](/us/usc/t7/s1508)” after “by the Board”.

1994—[Pub. L. 103–354](/us/pl/103/354) substituted “(m)” for “(k)” after “subsection (a) or”.

1991—[Pub. L. 102–237](/us/pl/102/237) substituted “subsection (a) or (k)” for “subsection (a) or (i)”.

1980—[Pub. L. 96–365](/us/pl/96/365) extended definition of “agricultural commodity” to include tomatoes, grain sorghum, sunflowers, raisins, oranges, sweet corn, dry peas, freezing and canning peas, forage, apples, grapes, nursery crops, and aquacultural species as illustrated but not limited, excluded livestock and stored grain, substituted “sugar cane” for “sugarcane”, and inserted reference to subsec. (i) of [section 1508 of this title](/us/usc/t7/s1508).

1949—Act Aug. 25, 1949, amended section to correct a clerical error in citation of “subsection (a) of section 1508”.

1944—Act Dec. 23, 1944, increased scope of definition of “agricultural commodity” from “wheat or cotton” to include all crops now set out.
