# §§ 1191 to 1202. Repealed. [Pub. L. 85–861, § 36A](/us/pl/85/861/s36A), Sept. 2, 1958, [72 Stat. 1569](/us/stat/72/1569)

##### §§ 1191 to 1202. Repealed. [Pub. L. 85–861, § 36A](/us/pl/85/861/s36A), Sept. 2, 1958, [72 Stat. 1569](/us/stat/72/1569)

Section 1191, acts [Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257), title II, § 201, [68 Stat. 1150](/us/stat/68/1150); [June 30, 1955, ch. 247, § 1(a)](/us/act/1955-06-30/ch247/s1/a), [69 Stat. 218](/us/stat/69/218), related to constructive service credit on initial appointment. See [section 12207 of Title 10](/us/usc/t10/s12207), Armed Forces, and [section 3738 of Title 14](/us/usc/t14/s3738), Coast Guard.

Section 1192, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 202](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s202), [68 Stat. 1150](/us/stat/68/1150), related to eligibility for promotion and to standards and qualifications for active status. See sections 12642 and 14301 et seq. of Title 10, Armed Forces, and [section 3743 of Title 14](/us/usc/t14/s3743), Coast Guard.

Section 1193, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 203](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s203), [68 Stat. 1150](/us/stat/68/1150), related to appointment, composition, duration of service, quorum, and oath of service of selection boards, and to communications by officers eligible for promotion. See section 14101 et seq. of Title 10, Armed Forces, and [section 3741 of Title 14](/us/usc/t14/s3741), Coast Guard.

Section 1194, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 204](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s204), [68 Stat. 1151](/us/stat/68/1151), authorized retention of officers with incomplete reserve service. See [section 12645 of Title 10](/us/usc/t10/s12645), Armed Forces.

Section 1195, acts [Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257), title II, § 205, [68 Stat. 1151](/us/stat/68/1151); [June 30, 1955, ch. 247, § 1(b)](/us/act/1955-06-30/ch247/s1/b), [69 Stat. 218](/us/stat/69/218), related to retention of officers with eighteen or more years of service. See [section 12646 of Title 10](/us/usc/t10/s12646).

Section 1196, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 206](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s206), [68 Stat. 1152](/us/stat/68/1152), provided for advancement in grade on retirement or transfer to Retired Reserve. See sections 12771 to 12773 of Title 10.

Section 1197, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 207](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s207), [68 Stat. 1152](/us/stat/68/1152), related to grade on entry upon active duty. See [section 12320 of Title 10](/us/usc/t10/s12320), Armed Forces, and [section 3756 of Title 14](/us/usc/t14/s3756), Coast Guard.

Section 1198, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 208](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s208), [68 Stat. 1152](/us/stat/68/1152), provided for recommendation for promotion of officer previously removed from active status. See [section 14317 of Title 10](/us/usc/t10/s14317), Armed Forces, and [section 3744 of Title 14](/us/usc/t14/s3744), Coast Guard.

Section 1199, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 209](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s209), [68 Stat. 1152](/us/stat/68/1152), authorized the President to suspend provisions of Reserve Officer Personnel Act of 1954 in time of war or national emergency. See [section 123 of Title 10](/us/usc/t10/s123), Armed Forces, [section 3733 of Title 14](/us/usc/t14/s3733), Coast Guard, and [section 111 of Title 32](/us/usc/t32/s111), National Guard.

Section 1200, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 210](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s210), [68 Stat. 1152](/us/stat/68/1152), provided that there shall be no requirement for sea or foreign service for promotion of Reserve officers.

Section 1201, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 211](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s211), [68 Stat. 1153](/us/stat/68/1153), related to grades of Reserve officers. See [section 12202 of Title 10](/us/usc/t10/s12202), Armed Forces.

Section 1202, [act Sept. 3, 1954, ch. 1257, title II, § 212](/us/act/1954-09-03/ch1257/tII/s212), [68 Stat. 1153](/us/stat/68/1153), related to active status of officers assigned to Selective Service System. See [section 12647 of Title 10](/us/usc/t10/s12647), Armed Forces.
