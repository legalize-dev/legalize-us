# § 5364. Prohibition against management interlocks between certain financial companies

##### § 5364. Prohibition against management interlocks between certain financial companies

A nonbank financial company supervised by the Board of Governors shall be treated as a bank holding company for purposes of the Depository Institutions 11 So in original. Probably should be “Institution”. Management Interlocks Act ([12 U.S.C. 3201](/us/usc/t12/s3201) et seq.), except that the Board of Governors shall not exercise the authority provided in section 7 22 So in original. There is no section 7 of such Act. of that Act ([12 U.S.C. 3207](/us/usc/t12/s3207)) to permit service by a management official of a nonbank financial company supervised by the Board of Governors as a management official of any bank holding company with total consolidated assets equal to or greater than $250,000,000,000, or other nonaffiliated nonbank financial company supervised by the Board of Governors (other than to provide a temporary exemption for interlocks resulting from a merger, acquisition, or consolidation).

*([Pub. L. 111–203, title I, § 164](/us/pl/111/203/tI/s164), July 21, 2010, [124 Stat. 1423](/us/stat/124/1423); [Pub. L. 115–174, title IV, § 401(c)(1)(F)](/us/pl/115/174/tIV/s401/c/1/F), May 24, 2018, [132 Stat. 1358](/us/stat/132/1358).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### References in Text

The Depository Institution Management Interlocks Act, referred to in text, is title II of [Pub. L. 95–630](/us/pl/95/630), Nov. 10, 1978, [92 Stat. 3672](/us/stat/92/3672), which is classified principally to chapter 33 (§ 3201 et seq.) of this title. For complete classification of this Act to the Code, see Short Title note set out under [section 3201 of this title](/us/usc/t12/s3201) and Tables.

#### Amendments

2018—[Pub. L. 115–174](/us/pl/115/174) substituted “$250,000,000,000” for “$50,000,000,000”.

#### **Statutory Notes and Related Subsidiaries**

#### Effective Date of 2018 Amendment

Except as otherwise provided, amendment by [Pub. L. 115–174](/us/pl/115/174) effective 18 months after May 24, 2018, see [section 401(d) of Pub. L. 115–174](/us/pl/115/174/s401/d), set out as a note under [section 5365 of this title](/us/usc/t12/s5365).
