# § 9282. Medals: posthumous award and presentation

##### § 9282. Medals: posthumous award and presentation

(a) If a person dies before the award of a medal of honor, distinguished-service cross, distinguished-service medal, distinguished flying cross, or device in place thereof, to which he is entitled, the award may be made and the medal or device presented to his representative, as designated by the President.

(b) If a person dies before an authorized service medal or device prescribed as a part thereof is presented to him under [section 9281 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s9281), it shall be presented to his family.

*([Aug. 10, 1956, ch. 1041](/us/act/1956-08-10/ch1041), [70A Stat. 542](/us/stat/70A/542), § 8752; [Pub. L. 85–861, § 33(a)(23)](/us/pl/85/861/s33/a/23), Sept. 2, 1958, [72 Stat. 1565](/us/stat/72/1565); renumbered § 9282 and amended [Pub. L. 115–232, div. A, title VIII](/us/pl/115/232/dA/tVIII), §§ 806(b)(11), 809(a), Aug. 13, 2018, [132 Stat. 1833](/us/stat/132/1833), 1840.)*

In subsection (a), the words “If a person” are substituted for the words “In case an individual * * * dies”, in 10:1409, and “In case an individual * * * shall have died”, in 10:1429. The words “within three years from the date”, in 10:1409, are omitted as covered by [section 8744 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s8744). The words “who shall distinguish himself”, in 10:1409, and “who distinguishes himself”, in 10:1429, are omitted as covered by the words “the award * * * to which he is entitled”.

#### 1958 Act

The change reflects the fact that the source statute for these sections (sec. 1 of the [Act of May 12, 1928, ch. 528](/us/act/1928-05-12/ch528), [45 Stat. 500](/us/stat/45/500)) was mandatory and not merely permissive.
