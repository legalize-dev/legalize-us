# §§ 61g–1 to 61g–3. Omitted

##### §§ 61g–1 to 61g–3. Omitted

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Codification

Section 61g–1, [Pub. L. 89–691, title IV, § 404](/us/pl/89/691/tIV/s404), Oct. 15, 1966, [80 Stat. 1024](/us/stat/80/1024), authorized, effective Oct. 1, 1966, Senate Majority Leader to fix the gross compensation of Secretary for Majority at not to exceed $25,611.05 per annum so long as position is held by present incumbent. See [section 6151 of this title](/us/usc/t2/s6151).

Sections 61g–2 and 61g–3, [Pub. L. 94–59, title I](/us/pl/94/59/tI), July 25, 1975, [89 Stat. 272](/us/stat/89/272), originally classified to section 61g–3 and later reclassified to section 61g–2, authorized, effective July 1, 1975, and each fiscal year thereafter, Secretaries for Senate Majority and Minority to each appoint and fix compensation of an assistant during emergencies at specified rates of compensation for not more than six months in each fiscal year. [Pub. L. 95–94, title I](/us/pl/95/94/tI), Aug. 5, 1977, [91 Stat. 658](/us/stat/91/658), abolished such positions, effective Oct. 1, 1977, and authorized Secretaries concerned to appoint such employees as they deem appropriate. See [section 6152 of this title](/us/usc/t2/s6152).
