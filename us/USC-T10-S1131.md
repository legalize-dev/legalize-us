# § 1131. Purple Heart: limitation to members of the armed forces

##### § 1131. Purple Heart: limitation to members of the armed forces

The decoration known as the Purple Heart (authorized to be awarded pursuant to Executive Order 11016) may only be awarded to a person who is a member of the armed forces at the time the person is killed or wounded under circumstances otherwise qualifying that person for award of the Purple Heart.

*(Added [Pub. L. 105–85, div. A, title V, § 571(a)(1)](/us/pl/105/85/dA/tV/s571/a/1), Nov. 18, 1997, [111 Stat. 1756](/us/stat/111/1756).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### References in Text

Executive Order 11016, referred to in text, is Ex. Ord. No. 11016, Apr. 25, 1962, 27 F.R. 4139, which is set out as a note under [section 1129 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s1129).

#### **Statutory Notes and Related Subsidiaries**

#### Effective Date

[Pub. L. 105–85, div. A, title V, § 571(b)](/us/pl/105/85/dA/tV/s571/b), Nov. 18, 1997, [111 Stat. 1756](/us/stat/111/1756), provided that: “[Section 1131 of title 10](/us/usc/t10/s1131), United States Code, as added by subsection (a), shall apply with respect to persons who are killed or wounded after the end of the 180-day period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act [Nov. 18, 1997].”
