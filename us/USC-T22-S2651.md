# § 2651. Establishment of Department

##### § 2651. Establishment of Department

There shall be at the seat of government an executive department to be known as the “Department of State”, and a Secretary of State, who shall be the head thereof.

*(R.S. § 199.)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Codification

R.S. § 199 derived from acts [July 27, 1789, ch. 4, § 1](/us/act/1789-07-27/ch4/s1), [1 Stat. 28](/us/stat/1/28); [Sept. 15, 1789, ch. 14, § 1](/us/act/1789-09-15/ch14/s1), [1 Stat. 68](/us/stat/1/68).

Section was formerly classified to [section 151 of Title 5](/us/usc/t5/s151) prior to the general revision and enactment of Title 5, Government Organization and Employees, by [Pub. L. 89–554, § 1](/us/pl/89/554/s1), Sept. 6, 1966, [80 Stat. 378](/us/stat/80/378).

#### Challenges to Appointment and Continuance in Office of Secretary of State First Appointed After May 3, 1980; Constitutional Provisions Governing Compensation and Emoluments

[Pub. L. 96–241, § 2](/us/pl/96/241/s2), May 3, 1980, [94 Stat. 343](/us/stat/94/343), provided that: “(a) Any person aggrieved by an action of the Secretary of State may bring a civil action in an appropriate United States district court to contest the constitutionality of the appointment and continuance in office of the Secretary of State on the ground that such appointment and continuance in office is in violation of article I, section 6, clause 2, of the Constitution. The United States district courts shall have exclusive jurisdiction, without regard to the sum or value of the matter in controversy, to determine the validity of such appointment and continuance in office. “(b) Any action brought under this section shall be heard and determined by a panel of three judges in accordance with [section 2284 of title 28](/us/usc/t28/s2284), United States Code. Any review of the action of a court convened pursuant to such section shall be by petition of certiorari to the Supreme Court. “(c) Any judge designated to hear any action brought under this section shall cause such action to be in every way expedited. “(d) This section applies only with respect to the Secretary of State who is first appointed to that office after the enactment of this Act [May 3, 1980].”
