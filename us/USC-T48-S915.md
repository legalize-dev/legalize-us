# § 915. Bonds as public debt

##### § 915. Bonds as public debt

Such bonds shall not be a debt of Puerto Rico or any municipality, and shall not constitute a public indebtedness within the meaning of section 3 of the Act of Congress approved March 2, 1917, entitled “An Act to provide a civil government for Porto Rico, and for other purposes”, as amended.

*([June 25, 1938, ch. 703, § 5](/us/act/1938-06-25/ch703/s5), [52 Stat. 1203](/us/stat/52/1203).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### References in Text

Section 3 of the Act of Congress approved March 2, 1917, referred to in text, is section 3 of [act Mar. 2, 1917, ch. 145](/us/act/1917-03-02/ch145), [39 Stat. 953](/us/stat/39/953). The provisions of section 3 of the Act limiting the public indebtedness of Puerto Rico and of certain municipalities thereof were contained in [section 745 of this title](/us/usc/t48/s745), prior to the amendment made to that section by [Pub. L. 87–121, § 1](/us/pl/87/121/s1), Aug. 3, 1961, [75 Stat. 245](/us/stat/75/245), which struck out those provisions. See 1961 Amendment note under [section 745 of this title](/us/usc/t48/s745).

#### Codification

Section was not enacted as part of the Puerto Rican Federal Relations Act which comprises this chapter.
