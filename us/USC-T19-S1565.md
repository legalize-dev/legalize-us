# § 1565. Cartage

##### § 1565. Cartage

(1) cartmen appointed and licensed by the Customs Service; or

(2) carriers designated under [section 1551 of this title](/us/usc/t19/s1551) to carry bonded merchandise;

*([June 17, 1930, ch. 497](/us/act/1930-06-17/ch497), title IV, § 565, [46 Stat. 747](/us/stat/46/747); [Pub. L. 91–271, title III, § 301(x)](/us/pl/91/271/tIII/s301/x), June 2, 1970, [84 Stat. 290](/us/stat/84/290); [Pub. L. 103–182, title VI, § 666](/us/pl/103/182/tVI/s666), Dec. 8, 1993, [107 Stat. 2215](/us/stat/107/2215).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Prior Provisions

Provisions similar to those in this section were contained in [act Sept. 21, 1922, ch. 356, title IV, § 565](/us/act/1922-09-21/ch356/tIV/s565), [42 Stat. 979](/us/stat/42/979). That section was superseded by section 565 of act June 17, 1930, comprising this section, and repealed by section 651(a)(1) of the 1930 act.

[Act June 22, 1874, ch. 391, § 25](/us/act/1874-06-22/ch391/s25), [18 Stat. 191](/us/stat/18/191), required cartage of merchandise in the custody of the government to be let to the lowest responsible bidder, prior to repeal by [act Sept. 21, 1922, ch. 356, title IV, § 643](/us/act/1922-09-21/ch356/tIV/s643), [42 Stat. 989](/us/stat/42/989).

#### Amendments

1993—[Pub. L. 103–182](/us/pl/103/182) amended first sentence generally. Prior to amendment, first sentence read as follows: “The cartage of merchandise entered for warehouse shall be done by cartmen to be appointed and licensed by the appropriate customs officer and who shall give a bond in a penal sum to be fixed by such customs officer, for the protection of the Government against any loss of, or damage to, such merchandise while being so carted.”

1970—[Pub. L. 91–271](/us/pl/91/271) substituted references to appropriate customs officer of customs officer for references to collector of customs or collector wherever appearing.
