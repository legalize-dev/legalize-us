# § 2520. Military training and service exemption

##### § 2520. Military training and service exemption

Notwithstanding the provisions of any other law or regulation, service in the Peace Corps as a volunteer shall not in any way exempt such volunteer from the performance of any obligations or duties under the provisions of the Universal Military Training and Service Act [[50 U.S.C. 3801](/us/usc/t50/s3801) et seq.].

*([Pub. L. 87–293, title I, § 23](/us/pl/87/293/tI/s23), Sept. 22, 1961, [75 Stat. 624](/us/stat/75/624).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### References in Text

The Universal Military Training and Service Act, referred to in text, subsequently renamed the Military Selective Service Act, is [act June 24, 1948, ch. 625](/us/act/1948-06-24/ch625), [62 Stat. 604](/us/stat/62/604), which is classified principally to chapter 49 (§ 3801 et seq.) of Title 50, War and National Defense. For complete classification of this Act to the Code, see Tables.
