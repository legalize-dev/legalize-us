# §§ 1651 to 1656. Repealed. [Pub. L. 106–224, title IV, § 438(a)(7)](/us/pl/106/224/tIV/s438/a/7), June 20, 2000, [114 Stat. 454](/us/stat/114/454)

##### §§ 1651 to 1656. Repealed. [Pub. L. 106–224, title IV, § 438(a)(7)](/us/pl/106/224/tIV/s438/a/7), June 20, 2000, [114 Stat. 454](/us/stat/114/454)

Section 1651, [act July 14, 1952, ch. 721, § 2](/us/act/1952-07-14/ch721/s2), [66 Stat. 597](/us/stat/66/597), set forth Federal Government policy to control, suppress, and eradicate the poisonous weed Halogeton glomeratus.

Section 1652, [act July 14, 1952, ch. 721, § 3](/us/act/1952-07-14/ch721/s3), [66 Stat. 598](/us/stat/66/598), set forth provisions relating to authority of Secretaries of Agriculture and the Interior and requiring consent prior to conducting measures and operations to control, suppress, or eradicate the weed.

Section 1653, [act July 14, 1952, ch. 721, § 4](/us/act/1952-07-14/ch721/s4), [66 Stat. 598](/us/stat/66/598), authorized discretionary expenditure of funds and acceptance of voluntary and uncompensated services of Federal, State, and local employees.

Section 1654, [act July 14, 1952, ch. 721, § 5](/us/act/1952-07-14/ch721/s5), [66 Stat. 598](/us/stat/66/598), gave Secretaries discretion to require contributions by States as prerequisites to making Federal expenditures.

Section 1655, [act July 14, 1952, ch. 721, § 6](/us/act/1952-07-14/ch721/s6), [66 Stat. 598](/us/stat/66/598), authorized appropriations and set forth provisions relating to their use.

Section 1656, [act July 14, 1952, ch. 721, § 7](/us/act/1952-07-14/ch721/s7), [66 Stat. 599](/us/stat/66/599), set forth provisions relating to extent of authority of this chapter.

#### **Statutory Notes and Related Subsidiaries**

#### Short Title

[Act July 14, 1952, ch. 721, § 1](/us/act/1952-07-14/ch721/s1), [66 Stat. 597](/us/stat/66/597), which provided that this chapter could be cited as the “Halogeton Glomeratus Control Act”, was repealed by [Pub. L. 106–224, title IV, § 438(a)(7)](/us/pl/106/224/tIV/s438/a/7), June 20, 2000, [114 Stat. 454](/us/stat/114/454).
