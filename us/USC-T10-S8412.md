# § 8412. Aviation cadets: benefits

##### § 8412. Aviation cadets: benefits

Except as provided in section 402(a) and (b) of title 37, aviation cadets or their beneficiaries are entitled to the same allowances, pensions, gratuities, and other benefits as are provided for enlisted members in pay grade E–4. While on active duty, an aviation cadet is entitled to uniforms, clothing, and equipment at the expense of the United States.

*([Aug. 10, 1956, ch. 1041](/us/act/1956-08-10/ch1041), [70A Stat. 426](/us/stat/70A/426), § 6912; [Pub. L. 85–861, § 1(147)(A)](/us/pl/85/861/s1/147/A), Sept. 2, 1958, [72 Stat. 1513](/us/stat/72/1513); [Pub. L. 87–649, § 6(f)(6)](/us/pl/87/649/s6/f/6), Sept. 7, 1962, [76 Stat. 494](/us/stat/76/494); renumbered § 8412, [Pub. L. 115–232, div. A, title VIII, § 807(c)(1)](/us/pl/115/232/dA/tVIII/s807/c/1), Aug. 13, 2018, [132 Stat. 1836](/us/stat/132/1836).)*

In subsection (b) the words “and at the same rates” are omitted as covered by the words “on the same basis”.

In subsection (c) the words “enlisted members in pay grade E–4” are substituted for “enlisted men of the fourth pay grade” to conform to the terminology of the Career Compensation Act of 1949 ([37 U.S.C. 231](/us/usc/t37/s231) et seq.). The words “by law or regulation” are omitted as surplusage. The words “and the premiums on their life insurance” are omitted as impliedly repealed by § 10 of the Insurance Act of 1951, [65 Stat. 37](/us/stat/65/37), which provided that such premium payments shall not be made by the Government.

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Amendments

2018—[Pub. L. 115–232](/us/pl/115/232) renumbered [section 6912 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s6912) as this section.

1962—[Pub. L. 87–649](/us/pl/87/649) substituted “section 402(a) and (b) of title 37” for “[section 251(a) of title 37](/us/usc/t37/s251/a)”.

1958—[Pub. L. 85–861](/us/pl/85/861) substituted “benefits” for “pay and allowances” in section catchline, and struck out provisions which prescribed the rate of pay of cadets, which authorized them to receive the same allowances for subsistence as prescribed for officers, which related to the furnishing of quarters, medical care and hospitalization, and which authorized transportation and expenses while traveling under orders.
