# § 1506. Additional Assistant Secretary; appointment; applicability of section 1505

##### § 1506. Additional Assistant Secretary; appointment; applicability of section 1505

There shall be on and after July 2, 1954 in the Department of Commerce, in addition to the Assistant Secretaries now provided for by law, one additional Assistant Secretary of Commerce, who shall be appointed by the President by and with the advice and consent of the Senate, and who shall be subject in all respects to the provisions of [section 1505 of this title](/us/usc/t15/s1505), relating to Assistant Secretaries of Commerce.

*([July 2, 1954, ch. 456](/us/act/1954-07-02/ch456), title III, § 304, [68 Stat. 430](/us/stat/68/430).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Codification

Section constitutes the first sentence of section 304 of act July 2, 1954. The second sentence of such section 304 repealed section 3 of Reorg. Plan 5 of 1950, 15 F.R. 3174, [64 Stat. 1263](/us/stat/64/1263), set out as a note under [section 1501 of this title](/us/usc/t15/s1501), which established the position of Administrative Assistant Secretary of Commerce.

Section was formerly classified to [section 592a–3 of Title 5](/us/usc/t5/s592a–3) prior to the general revision and enactment of Title 5, Government Organization and Employees, by [Pub. L. 89–554](/us/pl/89/554), Sept. 6, 1966, [80 Stat. 378](/us/stat/80/378).
