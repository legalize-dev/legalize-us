# § 9682. Obsolete or excess material: sale to National Council of Boy Scouts of America

##### § 9682. Obsolete or excess material: sale to National Council of Boy Scouts of America

Subject to regulations under [section 121 of title 40](/us/usc/t40/s121), the Secretary of the Air Force, under such conditions as he may prescribe, may sell obsolete or excess material to the National Council of the Boy Scouts of America. Sales under this section shall be at fair value to the Department of the Air Force, including packing, handling, and transportation.

*([Aug. 10, 1956, ch. 1041](/us/act/1956-08-10/ch1041), [70A Stat. 583](/us/stat/70A/583); [Pub. L. 96–513, title V, § 514(18)](/us/pl/96/513/tV/s514/18), Dec. 12, 1980, [94 Stat. 2936](/us/stat/94/2936); [Pub. L. 107–217, § 3(b)(37)](/us/pl/107/217/s3/b/37), Aug. 21, 2002, [116 Stat. 1298](/us/stat/116/1298).)*

The words “obsolete or excess material” are substituted for the words “such obsolete material as may not be needed by the Department of the Army, and such other material as may be spared” to conform to the Federal Property and Administrative Services Act of 1949, as amended ([40 U.S.C. 471](/us/usc/t40/s471) et seq.). The words “in his discretion” are omitted as surplusage.
