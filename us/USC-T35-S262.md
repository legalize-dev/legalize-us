# § 262. Joint owners

##### § 262. Joint owners

In the absence of any agreement to the contrary, each of the joint owners of a patent may make, use, offer to sell, or sell the patented invention within the United States, or import the patented invention into the United States, without the consent of and without accounting to the other owners.

*([July 19, 1952, ch. 950](/us/act/1952-07-19/ch950), [66 Stat. 810](/us/stat/66/810); [Pub. L. 103–465, title V, § 533(b)(3)](/us/pl/103/465/tV/s533/b/3), Dec. 8, 1994, [108 Stat. 4989](/us/stat/108/4989).)*

#### Historical and Revision Notes

This section states a condition in existing law not expressed in the existing statutes.

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Amendments

1994—[Pub. L. 103–465](/us/pl/103/465) substituted “use, offer to sell, or sell” for “use or sell” and inserted “within the United States, or import the patented invention into the United States,” after “invention”.
