# § 941. Art. 141. Status

##### § 941. Art. 141. Status

There is a court of record known as the United States Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces. The court is established under article I of the Constitution. The court is located for administrative purposes only in the Department of Defense.

*(Added [Pub. L. 101–189, div. A, title XIII, § 1301(c)](/us/pl/101/189/dA/tXIII/s1301/c), Nov. 29, 1989, [103 Stat. 1570](/us/stat/103/1570); amended [Pub. L. 103–337, div. A, title IX, § 924(a)(2)](/us/pl/103/337/dA/tIX/s924/a/2), Oct. 5, 1994, [108 Stat. 2831](/us/stat/108/2831).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Amendments

1994—[Pub. L. 103–337](/us/pl/103/337) substituted “Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces” for “Court of Military Appeals”.

#### **Statutory Notes and Related Subsidiaries**

#### Change of Name

[Pub. L. 103–337, div. A, title IX, § 924(a)(1)](/us/pl/103/337/dA/tIX/s924/a/1), Oct. 5, 1994, [108 Stat. 2831](/us/stat/108/2831), provided that: “The United States Court of Military Appeals shall hereafter be known and designated as the United States Court of Appeals for the Armed Forces.”
