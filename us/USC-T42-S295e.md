# § 295e. Authorization of appropriations

##### § 295e. Authorization of appropriations

(a) In general For the purpose of carrying out this subpart, there is authorized to be appropriated $17,000,000 for each of fiscal years 2021 through 2025.

(b) Limitation regarding certain program In obligating amounts appropriated under subsection (a), the Secretary may not obligate more than 30 percent for carrying out [section 295b of this title](/us/usc/t42/s295b).

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Prior Provisions

A prior section 295e, [act July 1, 1944, ch. 373, title VII, § 768](/us/act/1944-07-01/ch373/tVII/s768), as added Dec. 25, 1970, [Pub. L. 91–696, § 101](/us/pl/91/696/s101), [84 Stat. 2080–5](/us/stat/84/2080-5), set forth definitions for former part D of this subchapter, prior to repeal by [Pub. L. 99–129, title II, § 220(c)](/us/pl/99/129/tII/s220/c), Oct. 22, 1985, [99 Stat. 544](/us/stat/99/544).

Another prior section 295e consisted of section 766 of act July 1, 1944. The classification of section 766 of act July 1, 1944, was changed to [section 295d–1 of this title](/us/usc/t42/s295d–1) for purposes of codification.

Prior sections 295e–1 to 295e–5 were repealed by [Pub. L. 99–129, title II, § 220(c)](/us/pl/99/129/tII/s220/c), Oct. 22, 1985, [99 Stat. 544](/us/stat/99/544).

Section 295e–1, [act July 1, 1944, ch. 373, title VII, § 767](/us/act/1944-07-01/ch373/tVII/s767), as added Nov. 18, 1971, [Pub. L. 92–157, title I, § 107(b)](/us/pl/92/157/tI/s107/b), [85 Stat. 457](/us/stat/85/457); amended Oct. 12, 1976, [Pub. L. 94–484, title I, § 101(g)](/us/pl/94/484/tI/s101/g), [90 Stat. 2244](/us/stat/90/2244), authorized appropriations for grants to public or nonprofit private hospitals for training, traineeships, and fellowships in family medicine.

Section 295e–2, [act July 1, 1944, ch. 373, title VII, § 768](/us/act/1944-07-01/ch373/tVII/s768), as added Nov. 18, 1971, [Pub. L. 92–157, title I, § 107(b)](/us/pl/92/157/tI/s107/b), [85 Stat. 458](/us/stat/85/458); amended Oct. 12, 1976, [Pub. L. 94–484, title I, § 101(h)](/us/pl/94/484/tI/s101/h), [90 Stat. 2244](/us/stat/90/2244), established grants for postgraduate training programs for physicians and dentists and authorized appropriations for those grants.

Section 295e–3, [act July 1, 1944, ch. 373, title VII, § 769](/us/act/1944-07-01/ch373/tVII/s769), as added Nov. 18, 1971, [Pub. L. 92–157, title I, § 107(b)](/us/pl/92/157/tI/s107/b), [85 Stat. 459](/us/stat/85/459); amended Oct. 12, 1976, [Pub. L. 94–484, title I, § 101(i)](/us/pl/94/484/tI/s101/i), [90 Stat. 2245](/us/stat/90/2245), authorized the Secretary to make grants for training, traineeships, and fellowships for health professions teaching personnel and authorized appropriations for those grants.

Section 295e–4, [act July 1, 1944, ch. 373, title VII, § 769A](/us/act/1944-07-01/ch373/tVII/s769A), as added Nov. 18, 1971, [Pub. L. 92–157, title I, § 107(b)](/us/pl/92/157/tI/s107/b), [85 Stat. 459](/us/stat/85/459); amended Oct. 12, 1976, [Pub. L. 94–484, title I, § 101(j)](/us/pl/94/484/tI/s101/j), [90 Stat. 2245](/us/stat/90/2245), authorized appropriations for grants for computer technology health care demonstration programs.

Section 295e–5, [act July 1, 1944, ch. 373, title VII, § 769B](/us/act/1944-07-01/ch373/tVII/s769B), as added Nov. 18, 1971, [Pub. L. 92–157, title I, § 107(b)](/us/pl/92/157/tI/s107/b), [85 Stat. 460](/us/stat/85/460), required applications for grants and approval of grants by Secretary and set forth payment limitations.

#### Amendments

2020—Subsec. (a). [Pub. L. 116–136](/us/pl/116/136) substituted “$17,000,000 for each of fiscal years 2021 through 2025” for “$43,000,000 for fiscal year 2011, and such sums as may be necessary for each of the fiscal years 2012 through 2015”.

2010—Subsec. (a). [Pub. L. 111–148](/us/pl/111/148) amended subsec. (a) generally. Prior to amendment, text read as follows: “For the purpose of carrying out this subpart, there is authorized to be appropriated $9,100,000 for fiscal year 1998, and such sums as may be necessary for each of the fiscal years 1999 through 2002.”
