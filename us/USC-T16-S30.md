# § 30. Jail building; office of magistrate judge

##### § 30. Jail building; office of magistrate judge

The Secretary of the Interior shall cause to be erected in Yellowstone National Park a suitable building to be used as a jail, and also having in said building an office for the use of the United States magistrate judge.

*([May 7, 1894, ch. 72, § 9](/us/act/1894-05-07/ch72/s9), [28 Stat. 75](/us/stat/28/75); [Pub. L. 90–578, title IV, § 402(b)(2)](/us/pl/90/578/tIV/s402/b/2), Oct. 17, 1968, [82 Stat. 1118](/us/stat/82/1118); [Pub. L. 101–650, title III, § 321](/us/pl/101/650/tIII/s321), Dec. 1, 1990, [104 Stat. 5117](/us/stat/104/5117).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Codification

[Section 9 of the act of May 7, 1894](/us/act/1894-05-07/s9), contained the added clause, “the cost of such building not to exceed five thousand dollars, to be paid out of any moneys in the Treasury not otherwise appropriated upon certificate of the Secretary as a voucher therefor,” which was superseded by the provisions contained in [section 451 of this title](/us/usc/t16/s451).

#### **Statutory Notes and Related Subsidiaries**

#### Change of Name

“United States magistrate judge” substituted in text for “magistrate” pursuant to [section 321 of Pub. L. 101–650](/us/pl/101/650/s321), set out as a note under [section 631 of Title 28](/us/usc/t28/s631), Judiciary and Judicial Procedure. Previously, “magistrate” substituted for “commissioner” pursuant to [Pub. L. 90–578](/us/pl/90/578). See chapter 43 (§ 631 et seq.) of Title 28.
