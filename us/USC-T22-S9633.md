# § 9633. Maximum contingent liability

##### § 9633. Maximum contingent liability

(a) In general The maximum contingent liability of the Corporation outstanding at any one time shall not exceed in the aggregate $205,000,000,000.

(b) Rule of construction The maximum contingent liability shall apply to all extension of liability by the Corporation regardless of the authority cited thereto.

*([Pub. L. 115–254, div. F, title III, § 1433](/us/pl/115/254/dF/tIII/s1433), Oct. 5, 2018, [132 Stat. 3501](/us/stat/132/3501); [Pub. L. 119–60, div. H, title LXXXVII, § 8753](/us/pl/119/60/dH/tLXXXVII/s8753), Dec. 18, 2025, [139 Stat. 1964](/us/stat/139/1964).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Amendments

2025—[Pub. L. 119–60](/us/pl/119/60) amended section generally. Prior to amendment, text of section read as follows: “The maximum contingent liability of the Corporation outstanding at any one time shall not exceed in the aggregate $60,000,000,000.”
