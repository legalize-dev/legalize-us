# § 812. Art. 12. Prohibition of confinement of members of the armed forces with enemy prisoners and certain others

##### § 812. Art. 12. Prohibition of confinement of members of the armed forces with enemy prisoners and certain others

(1) enemy prisoners; or

(2) other individuals—

(A) who are detained under the law of war and are foreign nationals; and

(B) who are not members of the armed forces.

*([Aug. 10, 1956, ch. 1041](/us/act/1956-08-10/ch1041), [70A Stat. 41](/us/stat/70A/41); [Pub. L. 114–328, div. E, title LII, § 5122](/us/pl/114/328/dE/tLII/s5122), Dec. 23, 2016, [130 Stat. 2896](/us/stat/130/2896).)*

The words “of the United States” are omitted as surplusage. The word “may” is substituted for the word “shall”.

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Amendments

2016—[Pub. L. 114–328](/us/pl/114/328) amended section generally. Prior to amendment, text read as follows: “No member of the armed forces may be placed in confinement in immediate association with enemy prisoners or other foreign nationals not members of the armed forces.”
