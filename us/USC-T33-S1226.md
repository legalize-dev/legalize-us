# § 1226. Transferred

##### § 1226. Transferred

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Codification

Section was comprised of [Pub. L. 92–340, § 7](/us/pl/92/340/s7), as added [Pub. L. 99–399, title IX, § 906](/us/pl/99/399/tIX/s906), Aug. 27, 1986, as amended. Subsections (a) and (b) of [section 7 of Pub. L. 92–340](/us/pl/92/340/s7) were redesignated and transferred to [section 70116 of Title 46](/us/usc/t46/s70116), Shipping, by [Pub. L. 115–282, title IV, § 402(b)(1)](/us/pl/115/282/tIV/s402/b/1), Dec. 4, 2018, [132 Stat. 4264](/us/stat/132/4264). Subsection (c) of [section 7 of Pub. L. 92–340](/us/pl/92/340/s7) was redesignated subsec. (f) and transferred to [section 70103 of Title 46](/us/usc/t46/s70103) by [Pub. L. 115–282, title IV, § 402(c)(1)](/us/pl/115/282/tIV/s402/c/1), Dec. 4, 2018, [132 Stat. 4264](/us/stat/132/4264). [Pub. L. 115–282, title IV, § 408(a)](/us/pl/115/282/tIV/s408/a), (d)(1), Dec. 4, 2018, [132 Stat. 4268](/us/stat/132/4268), concurrently redesignated and transferred subsections (a) and (b) of [section 7 of Pub. L. 92–340](/us/pl/92/340/s7) to [section 70102a of Title 46](/us/usc/t46/s70102a) and identically redesignated subsection (c) of [section 7 of Pub. L. 92–340](/us/pl/92/340/s7) as subsec. (f) and transferred it to [section 70103 of Title 46](/us/usc/t46/s70103). However, [Pub. L. 116–283, div. G, title LVXXXV](/us/pl/116/283/dG/tLVXXXV) [LXXXV], § 8507(d)(1), (7), Jan. 1, 2021, [134 Stat. 4754](/us/stat/134/4754), subsequently repealed [section 408 of Pub. L. 115–282](/us/pl/115/282/s408), effective on the date of enactment of [Pub. L. 115–282](/us/pl/115/282) (Dec. 4, 2018), and provided that the provisions of law redesignated, transferred, or otherwise amended by such section 408 were amended to read as if that section had not been enacted.

#### Prior Provisions

A prior section 1226, [Pub. L. 92–340, § 7](/us/pl/92/340/s7), formerly title I, § 106, July 10, 1972, [86 Stat. 427](/us/stat/86/427); renumbered § 7 and amended [Pub. L. 95–474, § 2](/us/pl/95/474/s2), Oct. 17, 1978, [92 Stat. 1475](/us/stat/92/1475), related to requirement respecting federally licensed pilots on any foreign or domestic self-propelled vessel engaged in the foreign trade when operating in the navigable waters of the United States in areas, etc., where a pilot is not otherwise required by State law, prior to repeal by [Pub. L. 98–557, § 29(g)](/us/pl/98/557/s29/g), Oct. 30, 1984, [98 Stat. 2875](/us/stat/98/2875).
