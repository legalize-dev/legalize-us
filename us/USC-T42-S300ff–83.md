# § 300ff–83. Public health emergency

##### § 300ff–83. Public health emergency

(a) In general In an emergency area and during an emergency period, the Secretary shall have the authority to waive such requirements of this subchapter to improve the health and safety of those receiving care under this subchapter and the general public, except that the Secretary may not expend more than 5 percent of the funds allocated under this subchapter for sections 300ff–29a of this title and section 11 So in original. The word “section” probably should not appear. 300ff–13(b) of this title.

(b) Emergency area and emergency period In this section:

(1) Emergency area The term “emergency area” means a geographic area in which there exists—

(A) an emergency or disaster declared by the President pursuant to the National Emergencies Act [[50 U.S.C. 1601](/us/usc/t50/s1601) et seq.] or the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act [[42 U.S.C. 5121](/us/usc/t42/s5121) et seq.]; or

(B) a public health emergency declared by the Secretary pursuant to [section 247d of this title](/us/usc/t42/s247d).

(2) Emergency period The term “emergency period” means the period in which there exists—

(A) an emergency or disaster declared by the President pursuant to the National Emergencies Act or the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act; or

(B) a public health emergency declared by the Secretary pursuant to [section 247d of this title](/us/usc/t42/s247d).

(c) Unobligated funds If funds under a grant under this section are not expended for an emergency in the fiscal year in which the emergency is declared, such funds shall be returned to the Secretary for reallocation under sections 300ff–13(b) and 300ff–29a of this title.
