# § 289g–3. Breast implant research

##### § 289g–3. Breast implant research

(a) In general The Director of NIH may conduct or support research to examine the long-term health implications of silicone breast implants, both gel and saline filled. Such research studies may include the following:

(1) Developing and examining techniques to measure concentrations of silicone in body fluids and tissues.

(2) Surveillance of recipients of silicone breast implants, including long-term outcomes and local complications.

(b) Definition For purposes of this section, the term “breast implant” means a breast prosthesis that is implanted to augment or reconstruct the female breast.

*([July 1, 1944, ch. 373](/us/act/1944-07-01/ch373), title IV, § 498C, as added [Pub. L. 107–250, title II, § 215(b)](/us/pl/107/250/tII/s215/b), Oct. 26, 2002, [116 Stat. 1615](/us/stat/116/1615).)*
