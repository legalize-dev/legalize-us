# §§ 1023b to 1027. Repealed. [Pub. L. 92–181, title V, § 5](/us/pl/92/181/tV/s5).26(a), Dec. 10, 1971, [85 Stat. 624](/us/stat/85/624)

##### §§ 1023b to 1027. Repealed. [Pub. L. 92–181, title V, § 5](/us/pl/92/181/tV/s5).26(a), Dec. 10, 1971, [85 Stat. 624](/us/stat/85/624)

Section 1023b, [act July 26, 1956, ch. 741, title II, § 201(b)](/us/act/1956-07-26/ch741/tII/s201/b), [70 Stat. 667](/us/stat/70/667), provided for utilization of funds for administrative expenses of intermediate credit banks.

Section 1024, [act July 17, 1916, ch. 245, title II, § 201(d)](/us/act/1916-07-17/ch245/tII/s201/d), as added [Mar. 4, 1923, ch. 252](/us/act/1923-03-04/ch252), title I, § 2, [42 Stat. 1454](/us/stat/42/1454), authorized intermediate credit banks to serve as fiscal agents for the United States.

Section 1025, [act July 17, 1916, ch. 245, title II, § 201(e)](/us/act/1916-07-17/ch245/tII/s201/e), as added [Mar. 4, 1923, ch. 252](/us/act/1923-03-04/ch252), title I, § 2, [42 Stat. 1454](/us/stat/42/1454); amended Ex. Ord. No. 6084, Mar. 27, 1933; Aug. 18, 1959, [Pub. L. 86–168, title I, § 104(h)](/us/pl/86/168/tI/s104/h), [73 Stat. 387](/us/stat/73/387), covered insolvency and receivership of intermediate credit banks.

Section 1026, [act July 17, 1916, ch. 245, title II, § 201(f)](/us/act/1916-07-17/ch245/tII/s201/f), as added [Mar. 4, 1923, ch. 252](/us/act/1923-03-04/ch252), title I, § 2, [42 Stat. 1454](/us/stat/42/1454); amended Ex. Ord. No. 6084, Mar. 27, 1933, provided for grant of charters to intermediate credit banks upon application in form prescribed by Farm Credit Administration.

Section 1027, [act July 26, 1956, ch. 741, title I, § 101(a)](/us/act/1956-07-26/ch741/tI/s101/a) to (c), [70 Stat. 659](/us/stat/70/659), covered merger of production credit corporations in Federal intermediate credit banks.

Section 1027 note, [act July 26, 1956, ch. 741, § 2](/us/act/1956-07-26/ch741/s2), [70 Stat. 659](/us/stat/70/659), set out a Congressional declaration of policy to be followed in construing provisions of act July 26, 1956.
