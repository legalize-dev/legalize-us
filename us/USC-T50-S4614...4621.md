#### **Statutory Notes and Related Subsidiaries**

#### Application of Certain Provisions of Export Administration Act of 1979

[Pub. L. 113–276, title II, § 209](/us/pl/113/276/tII/s209), Dec. 18, 2014, [128 Stat. 2994](/us/stat/128/2994), provided that: “(a) Protection of Information.—Section 12(c) of the Export Administration Act of 1979 (50 U.S.C. App. 2411(c)) [former [50 U.S.C. 4614(c)](/us/usc/t50/s4614/c)] has been in effect from August 20, 2001, and continues in effect on and after the date of the enactment of this Act [Dec. 18, 2014], pursuant to the International Emergency Economic Powers Act ([50 U.S.C. 1701](/us/usc/t50/s1701) et seq.) and notwithstanding section 20 of the Export Administration Act of 1979 (50 U.S.C. App. 2419) [former [50 U.S.C. 4622](/us/usc/t50/s4622)]. Section 12(c)(1) of the Export Administration Act of 1979 is a statute covered by [section 552(b)(3) of title 5](/us/usc/t5/s552/b/3), United States Code. “(b) Termination Date.—Subsection (a) terminates at the end of the 4-year period beginning on the date of the enactment of this Act [Dec. 18, 2014].”
