# § 422a. Acceptance of lands

##### § 422a. Acceptance of lands

The establishment of the Moores Creek National Battlefield shall be carried forward under the control and direction of the Secretary of the Interior, who is authorized to receive from the State of North Carolina a deed of conveyance to the United States of all the lands belonging to the said State, embracing thirty acres, more or less, and described more particularly in [section 422 of this title](/us/usc/t16/s422).

*([June 2, 1926, ch. 448, § 2](/us/act/1926-06-02/ch448/s2), [44 Stat. 685](/us/stat/44/685); Ex. Ord. No. 6166, § 2, June 10, 1933; Ex. Ord. No. 6228, § 1, July 28, 1933; [Pub. L. 96–344, § 12](/us/pl/96/344/s12), Sept. 8, 1980, [94 Stat. 1136](/us/stat/94/1136).)*

#### **Statutory Notes and Related Subsidiaries**

#### Change of Name

“Battlefield” substituted in text for “Military Park” on authority of [Pub. L. 96–344, § 12](/us/pl/96/344/s12), Sept. 8, 1980, [94 Stat. 1136](/us/stat/94/1136), which redesignated Moores Creek National Military Park as Moores Creek National Battlefield.

#### **Executive Documents**

#### Transfer of Functions

Administrative functions of Moores Creek National Military Park transferred to Department of the Interior by Ex. Ord. Nos. 6166 and 6228, set out as a note under [section 901 of Title 5](/us/usc/t5/s901), Government Organization and Employees.
