# § 117. Oregon

##### § 117. Oregon

Oregon constitutes one judicial district.

Court shall be held at Coquille, Eugene or Springfield, Klamath Falls, Medford, Pendleton, and Portland.

*([June 25, 1948, ch. 646](/us/act/1948-06-25/ch646), [62 Stat. 888](/us/stat/62/888); [Aug. 3, 1950, ch. 514](/us/act/1950-08-03/ch514), [64 Stat. 393](/us/stat/64/393); [Pub. L. 91–272, § 7](/us/pl/91/272/s7), June 2, 1970, [84 Stat. 297](/us/stat/84/297); [Pub. L. 106–518, title V, § 502](/us/pl/106/518/tV/s502), Nov. 13, 2000, [114 Stat. 2422](/us/stat/114/2422).)*

#### Historical and Revision Notes

Based on title 28, U.S.C., 1940 ed., § 183 ([Mar. 3, 1911, ch. 231, § 102](/us/act/1911-03-03/ch231/s102), [36 Stat. 1122](/us/stat/36/1122); [Nov. 6, 1945, ch. 447](/us/act/1945-11-06/ch447), [59 Stat. 555](/us/stat/59/555)).

Provisions relating to appointment and residence of deputies by the clerk and marshal, and maintenance of offices by said officers, were omitted as covered by sections 541 [see 561], 542 [see 561], and 751 of this title.

Changes in arrangement and phraseology were made.

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Amendments

2000—[Pub. L. 106–518](/us/pl/106/518) substituted “Eugene or Springfield” for “Eugene”.

1970—[Pub. L. 91–272](/us/pl/91/272) provided for holding court at Coquille.

1950—Act Aug. 3, 1950, provided for holding court at Eugene.
