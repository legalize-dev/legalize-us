# § 157. Bankruptcy fraud

##### § 157. Bankruptcy fraud

(1) files a petition under title 11, including a fraudulent involuntary petition under section 303 of such title;

(2) files a document in a proceeding under title 11; or

(3) makes a false or fraudulent representation, claim, or promise concerning or in relation to a proceeding under title 11, at any time before or after the filing of the petition, or in relation to a proceeding falsely asserted to be pending under such title,

*(Added [Pub. L. 103–394, title III, § 312(a)(1)(B)](/us/pl/103/394/tIII/s312/a/1/B), Oct. 22, 1994, [108 Stat. 4140](/us/stat/108/4140); amended [Pub. L. 109–8, title III, § 332(c)](/us/pl/109/8/tIII/s332/c), Apr. 20, 2005, [119 Stat. 103](/us/stat/119/103); [Pub. L. 111–327, § 2(b)](/us/pl/111/327/s2/b), Dec. 22, 2010, [124 Stat. 3562](/us/stat/124/3562).)*

2010—Par. (1). [Pub. L. 111–327, § 2(b)(1)](/us/pl/111/327/s2/b/1), struck out “bankruptcy” after “involuntary”.

Pars. (2), (3). [Pub. L. 111–327, § 2(b)(2)](/us/pl/111/327/s2/b/2), struck out “, including a fraudulent involuntary bankruptcy petition under section 303 of such title” after “title 11”.

L. 109–8](/us/pl/109/8), which directed insertion of “, including a fraudulent involuntary bankruptcy petition under section 303 of such title” after “title 11”, was executed by making the insertion after “title 11” wherever appearing, to reflect the probable intent of Congress.
