# § 30144. Partial invalidity

##### § 30144. Partial invalidity

If any provision of this Act, or the application thereof to any person or circumstance, is held invalid, the validity of the remainder of the Act and the application of such provision to other persons and circumstances shall not be affected thereby.

*([Pub. L. 92–225, title IV, § 404](/us/pl/92/225/tIV/s404), Feb. 7, 1972, [86 Stat. 20](/us/stat/86/20).)*

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### References in Text

This Act, referred to in text, means the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971, as defined by [section 30101 of this title](/us/usc/t52/s30101).

#### Codification

Section was formerly classified to [section 454 of Title 2](/us/usc/t2/s454), The Congress, prior to editorial reclassification and renumbering as this section.

#### **Statutory Notes and Related Subsidiaries**

#### Severability

[Pub. L. 107–155, title IV, § 401](/us/pl/107/155/tIV/s401), Mar. 27, 2002, [116 Stat. 112](/us/stat/116/112), provided that: “If any provision of this Act [see Tables for classification] or amendment made by this Act, or the application of a provision or amendment to any person or circumstance, is held to be unconstitutional, the remainder of this Act and amendments made by this Act, and the application of the provisions and amendment to any person or circumstance, shall not be affected by the holding.”
