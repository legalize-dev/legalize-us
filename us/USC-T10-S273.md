# § 273. Training and advising civilian law enforcement officials

##### § 273. Training and advising civilian law enforcement officials

(1) to train Federal, State, and local civilian law enforcement officials in the operation and maintenance of equipment, including equipment made available under section 372 11 See References in Text note below. of this title; and

(2) to provide such law enforcement officials with expert advice relevant to the purposes of this chapter.

L. 99–145, title XIV, § 1423(a)](/us/pl/99/145/tXIV/s1423/a), Nov. 8, 1985, [99 Stat. 752](/us/stat/99/752); [Pub. L. 100–456, div. A, title XI, § 1104(a)](/us/pl/100/456/dA/tXI/s1104/a), Sept. 29, 1988, [102 Stat. 2043](/us/stat/102/2043); renumbered § 273, [Pub. L. 114–328, div. #### **Editorial Notes**

#### References in Text

[Section 372 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s372), referred to in par. (1), was renumbered [section 272 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s272) by [Pub. L. 114–328, div. A, title XII, § 1241(a)(2)](/us/pl/114/328/dA/tXII/s1241/a/2), Dec. 23, 2016, [130 Stat. 2497](/us/stat/130/2497).

#### Prior Provisions

A prior section 273, [act Aug. 10, 1956, ch. 1041](/us/act/1956-08-10/ch1041), [70A Stat. 13](/us/stat/70A/13), related to composition of Standby Reserve and maintenance of inactive status list in Standby Reserve, prior to repeal by [Pub. L. 103–337, div. A, title XVI](/us/pl/103/337/dA/tXVI), §§ 1661(a)(2)(A), 1691, Oct. 5, 1994, [108 Stat. 2979](/us/stat/108/2979), 3026, effective Dec. 1, 1994. See sections 10151 to 10153 of this title.

#### Amendments

2016—[Pub. L. 114–328](/us/pl/114/328) renumbered [section 373 of this title](/us/usc/t10/s373) as this section.

1988—[Pub. L. 100–456](/us/pl/100/456) amended section generally, substituting provisions authorizing Secretary of Defense, in accordance with applicable law, to make Defense Department personnel available for training, etc., for former subsecs. (a) to (c) authorizing Secretary of Defense to assign members of Army, Navy, Air Force, and Marine Corps, etc., for training, etc., briefing sessions by Attorney General, and other functions of Attorney General and Administrator of General Services.

1985—[Pub. L. 99–145](/us/pl/99/145) designated existing provisions as subsec. (a) and added subsecs. (b) and (c).
