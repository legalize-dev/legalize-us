# § 505. Solicitor General

##### § 505. Solicitor General

The President shall appoint in the Department of Justice, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate, a Solicitor General, learned in the law, to assist the Attorney General in the performance of his duties.

*(Added [Pub. L. 89–554, § 4(c)](/us/pl/89/554/s4/c), Sept. 6, 1966, [80 Stat. 612](/us/stat/80/612).)*

So much of R.S. § 347 as relates to the pay of the Solicitor General is omitted as superseded by [§ 303(c) of the Act of Aug. 14, 1964](/us/act/1964-08-14/s303/c), [Pub. L. 88–426](/us/pl/88/426), [78 Stat. 416](/us/stat/78/416), which is codified in [section 5314 of title 5](/us/usc/t5/s5314), United States Code.
