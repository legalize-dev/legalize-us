# § 1792. Mutiny and riot prohibited

##### § 1792. Mutiny and riot prohibited

Whoever instigates, connives, willfully attempts to cause, assists, or conspires to cause any mutiny or riot, at any Federal penal, detention, or correctional facility, shall be imprisoned not more than ten years or fined under this title, or both.

#### Historical and Revision Notes

Based on title 18, U.S.C., 1940 ed., § 252 ([May 18, 1934, ch. 303, § 1](/us/act/1934-05-18/ch303/s1), [48 Stat. 782](/us/stat/48/782)).

Escape provisions of this section were incorporated in [section 752 of this title](/us/usc/t18/s752).

Reference to persons causing, procuring, aiding and assisting was omitted. Such persons are principals under [section 2 of this title](/us/usc/t18/s2).

Minor changes were made in translation and phraseology.

#### **Editorial Notes**

#### Amendments

1994—[Pub. L. 103–322](/us/pl/103/322) substituted “fined under this title” for “fined not more than $25,000”.

1986—[Pub. L. 99–646](/us/pl/99/646) inserted “, detention,” after “penal”.

1984—[Pub. L. 98–473](/us/pl/98/473) substituted provisions deleting prohibition on bringing dangerous instrumentalities into prison and inserted provision setting forth a maximum $25,000 fine.
